Project Overview And Description

1. Topic:

The project that we plan to do involves creating an app that would help the population of blind, deaf, and mute people to be able to function on their own. The app would consist of 2 different features/options, one for the blind and one for the deaf-mute. Each would contain features that can specifically help each category of user to improve their everyday life.

First, for the blind and visually impaired people, the app would implement trained AI, machine learning, image recognition to analyze data collected through the phone’s camera. The app would be able differentiate objects, traffic signals, facial features, etc. and compile them into comprehensible descriptions to allow the users to be able to ‘see’ what they want to. Users can walk on their own with the app’s walking mode, which can be used to detect hazardous objects and traffic signals and signs to provide a safe walking session. Users can utilize the app to describe features of objects, sceneries, people that the camera detects. With continuous training of the AI, the app would constantly improve and give the users the most precise and detailed description. As a result, the number of features that can be added into the option is endless as the AI becomes more intelligent and is able to analyze more complicated subjects. The features can be executed via voice command and the assisting voice would be the phone virtual assistant (Siri, Google Assistant, etc.), which is integrated with the app to provide the best user experience.

Next, for the deaf-mute users, the app acts as sign language translator. With gesture recognition and AI, the app would analyze users’ gestures and translate them into precise and comprehensible verbal language. The app can also listen to what the users’ peers say and translate it back to sign languages. This allows an efficient two-way conversation for the users without any difficulty. There will be dictionaries of all the sign languages so users with different backgrounds can all use the app comfortably.

Overall, this app would be a tool for the users to gain back some of their lost senses and regain their independence as individuals, through the help of AI.

2. Motivation:

Despite that there is a large population of people who are visually impaired, deaf, and mute, they are often overlooked and forgotten. The market tends to only target and produce products and applications for people like us, who are fortunate to have all of our senses, to gain profit. Therefore, we would like to give back and help to improve any possible aspect of their lives. We truly think that everyone deserves the same benefits, so we are here to make that a possibility for these people.

Other than the drive to help the unfortunate, this project is also a chance for everyone to hone their developing skills as this is one ambitious task. This project includes multiple advanced technologies such as AI and machine learning, which is considered the future of the IT world. The project requires tedious and almost no-error-allowed execution. If we are able to successfully create the project, we would be up-to-date with state-of-the-art technology of AI and this can be a huge plus for our resume, with AI being the main attraction in the near future. With the rising demand for developers with AI knowledge, we would be the prioritized candidates for most companies.

3. Landscape:

As for goals, there are a few projects that are similar to ours. First, there is Be My Eyes, which is a free app that connects visually impaired people with volunteers through video calling for visual assistance. Despite that both projects provide help for sight, ours is much better in the sense that it operated independently and through AI. With Be My Eyes, users have to depend on the availability of helpers and network. What if there is no one that is accessible when the users need assistance? What if the users have no connection to the network? With our app, the AI is always available, and all the possible data are included during installation and is frequently updated, when there is access to the Internet. Therefore, with our project, the users would have much more independence compared to what our competitor provides. Next, for the sign language translation aspect of our project, there is one application called Hand Talk App, which can translate speech to sign language, which is displayed through a 3D human model. However, their project can only perform a 1-way translation. Despite that there are many promising projects, such as Gnosys and UNI, that have the same functions as ours, both of the projects’ status is unclear, whether they are postponed or were not able to succeed during their startup stage. So assuming if we succeed in our startup, the project would be the first of its kind.

Detail Description

❏ Aims:

Our main aim for this project is to successfully create a mobile app that can have features that would improve the lifestyle of the targeted users (blind, deaf, mute). People with these disabilities struggle a lot with their daily lives. Although people with amputated limbs or paralyzed also tremendously struggle with their everyday routine, there are still widely available options such as state-of-the-art prosthetic limbs and transporting inventions that can restore 90% of the functionality back to the users. However, with the current medical knowledge, people with their sight, hearing, and speech do not have many available tools to improve their living. Blind people can only wait for cornea donation to restore their vision; people with hearing loss can only opt to hearing aid, but hearing aid can only help so much since it can only partially restore the users’ hearing; and people who cannot speak are stuck with either sign language, writing mediums (note, messaging) to communicate. Therefore, this app is created to provide a cheap option to help people with the disabilities.

First, in order to make it a useful app, one of the most essential things to achieve is the AI component of the app. AI is the core to the success of the project. The projected AI is expected to accurately analyze and produce precise descriptions of what the camera captures and what the users request. It is needed in the translation component for the deaf and mute users. Without a perfected AI, the app would not be able to carry out such complex tasks. Therefore, the technology of machine learning and AI needs to be the most prioritized part of the project.

Next, since this app is for people who are unfortunate to have their senses taken away, the user interface needed to be easily comprehensible and simple for the users to be able to effortlessly use it. Even for people with all of their senses available, they still struggle to use something new, such as smartphones. This is usually caused by the combination of unfamiliarity to the technology and subpar UI design of it. As a result, it is a must, to design an adequate UI to help the users navigate around the app.

Finally, we need to have an accurate datapool for the sign languages to provide the most precise translation for the users. To do so, we must recruit experts in sign languages to help create a database of gestures and have them take part in the training of AI to ensure the quality of the translation. The quality control of the translation is crucial, since the users’ communication skill is limited so we need to make sure that their ‘voice’ is heard correctly.

Since his family loves to get involved with charities and participate in volunteer work, since a very young age, Duc has had the chance to interact with people of all circumstances. As a result, he grew a deep connection with the people and had tremendous compassion for them. Naturally, he would think of ways to better their unfortunate lives. To him, as for the poor, Duc needs a good amount of fund, which is out of his control during this age; for the amputees, there are already plenty of advanced medical and technological inventions that can support them; and with the population of people who is affected by Agent Orange, he has no capability to help them in any way. This left him with the population of blind and deaf-mute. He saw possible solutions that he can create with technology, to help them and has been compiling ideas for years. And when he has the chance to pursue IT as a career path and given the chance to execute a project, Duc is now finally able to assemble all of his cultivated thoughts and come up with Hachi. The name Hachi takes inspiration from the famous Shiba Inu Hachiko, a dog that is extremely smart and loyal to its owner, to convey that the created app would be an exceptionally dependable and helpful tool that would be with users on every step they take. When every member hears of Duc’s ideas for the project, everyone gives a nod and agrees to work on this together. That is how Hachi began.

Hachi is a mobile app that would provide the blind and deaf-mute more independence by giving back some of their senses. The app would have two different options: one for the blind, and one for the deaf-mute, which are optimized to provide the best experience for each category of user. First, for the blind users, the app will become their eyes, informing them of anything the phone’s camera can detect. This is possible with image recognition and AI. By implementing these technologies, the app can perform a diverse number of tasks for the users. The most essential thing that is taken from the blinds is their ability to travel confidently. With Hachi as their assistant, users can freely walk as the app can compute and detect hazardous obstacles and terrains, and notify the users of traffic signs and signals when walking. Thanks to machine learning and image recognition, AI can be trained by feeding databases of images of threats and traffic signals so that it can precisely detect them when encountered. The app can also identify human facial expression and emotion, distinguish human gestures, and recognize familiar faces. With facial recognition and gesture recognition, AI can be fed with data of all the possible facial features, gestures to be able to analyze the subjects when requested by the users. That is the most basic feature of the app, as with AI and recognition tools, the possibilities are endless. We are planning to teach the AI all the possible information available so that it can accurately distinguish everything. And with an implemented neural network, the AI would be able to describe objects more specifically, so that the users would be able visualize things much better rather than just getting a broad picture in their heads. The app would be able to describe the sceneries in front of the users, the atmosphere of the locations, correctly identify any objects and its characteristics, etc. When the option for the blind is selected, to make the experience as smooth as possible, the user would be taken to an interface where everything can be controlled by voice command. The app would implement the specific virtual assistant of each system (Siri, Google Assistant, Bixby) into the app, and any command would be replied with speech by the virtual assistants. To implement Siri, we can request for authorization to use Sirikit, which is used to implement app extensions that can be integrated with Siri; to implement Google Assistant, we can use Google’s App Actions, which allows developers to create a database of commands and add it into an existing app, to allow the users to be able to carry out their requests; for Bixby, Samsung has provided Bixby Developer Studio, which provides Bixby’s SDK and API to allow developers to implement the assistant into their apps.

Next, there is the option for the deaf-mute. In this option, the app would turn into a two-way translation app. Since most people of the community have to use sign language as their primary way of communication, we figured out that the best way for them to be able to function better is to allow them to be understood by people. With the app, the users can have the camera capture their sign signal gestures and they will be analyzed and translated into text or speech for people who the users converse with. To do this, we need to implement AI, gesture recognition and neural networks. First, we need to create a database of different sign languages gestures, which can be collected either from online databases or from self-collecting data from hired professional sign language interpreters. Then, we will have the AI learn gesture recognition and have fed with the sign language database. In combination with neural networks, the AI would be able to produce more complex and precise outputs. To be able to convert the text into speech, the AI would also be taught text-to-speech functions, and the speech would be carried out through the virtual assistant. Next, the users would also have the option to let the app translate back what their peers said into their default sign languages, which is conveyed through 3D hand models on the screen. For this feature, we need to apply AI, speech recognition, and neural networks. Initially, the AI is trained to perform speech-to-text to be able output text from speech. Then, since the AI has already learned how to translate from sign to text, with a neural network, the AI would be able to analyze the non-linear data and convert the collected text to signs.

As of now, due to the complexity of the project, the team has yet to complete anything regarding the actual app. We do not have the required knowledge to develop anything. However, we have every single detail planned out such as how the interface should look, the design of the app logo, where to obtain the needed data and technologies, etc. The only thing we lack is skill, which can be obtained gradually throughout our Bachelor program. Also, we have talked to multiple people in the industry to make sure that our plan is on the right track and what we have is proper for the project. Most of the experts judge that what we are doing and have planned is perfectly fine and correct. However, there are some concerns that we should consider. First, the app, due to its large amount of data and complexity, would take too much storage and phones’ energy, which can be a big factor for some customers. As a result, we should make two different apps, one for the blind and one for the deaf-mute, in order to downsize the app size and its toll on the CPU of the phones. This is something we will reconsider and will execute if our final product turns out to be what the professionals have predicted. There is one other common warning from the experts. With all the technologies involved, this project can be quite expensive to make, and due to the nature of it not being a profitable product and having a low success rate, our startup might have extreme difficulty in finding sponsors and investors. However, we still have hopes from non-profit organizations, since what we are doing can be a big innovation to better the public benefit.

With everything said, we will continue to work hard on this project. We will work hard to improve our skills as developers to reach the needed potential for the complexity of the app.

Skills And Jobs

AI is a new technology nowadays and many modern countries all over the world are trying to develop and manipulate this new technology into our daily life and works. But this technology required a lot of skills in order to work with it. Therefore, my group decided to divide into 4 different sections for each member.

• Data collector: The first thing that every project need is data. So, there will be one member in our group who will do this job. Our project is for people with disabilities like we have said above, therefore the data that we collected will be related to disabilities. For example, for the deaf-mute, they always use sign language in order to communicate with other people. So, the picture about sign language is what we need to train our AI system to work. For people who are blind, we will design our system differently, our application will describe in detail what is in front of the camera. Therefore, the user can know what the views in front of them are. So, the job of the collector is researching and collecting as many pictures as possible in order to train our AI to describe the view correctly.

• Application designer: Another job that is also important to our project is designing the interface of the application. Every application or every project will need people to design the user interface. The role of the interface is to attract more customers to use the application. Therefore, this role is for those who feel familiar with C/C++, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, … Moreover, designing an application is also very important because it helps the user to understand or figure out the way to use the application. If the interface is too complicated, the user will feel uncomfortable or bored, so they will find other apps which are easier to use.

• AI/ System designer: This will be the core section of this project. It is very important because the behaviors of the application rely on how well the system is designed. So, the one who enrolls for this job must be familiar with some programming languages such as python, C/C++, R, JavaScript, … Moreover, in this section, we will import all the data of the collector into the application. He or she will also be the one to train the system to recognize things. Also, this section requires you to have a good knowledge in mathematics, algorithms especially about probability and statistics.

• Beta tester: This is the easiest section in this project. In this section, your job is to use our application and then write feedback for us. The feedback is about how you feel when using the app or the problems that you are facing when using it, … The feedback should be specific and detailed; therefore, our team could find out the solution to solve the problems. Also, it is important to have feedback because we can base it on updates and improve our service to all the users. So that our app can meet the requirement and make the user feel comfortable when using it.
